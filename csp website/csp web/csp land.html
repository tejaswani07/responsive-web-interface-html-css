NEED OF LAND

PROBLEM STATEMENT:The result is small farms, low production per person and increasing landlessness. A consequence of land shortage is poverty. Land shortage and poverty, taken together, lead to non-sustainable land management practices, the direct causes of degradation.

How can we increase the land under cultivation?

1.Intensive Farming: Intensive farming is a method of farming in which the land is cultivated to produce high yields per unit area. This method involves the use of modern technology, high-yielding crop varieties, and intensive use of inputs such as fertilizers, pesticides, and irrigation. Intensive farming can help to increase the productivity of existing farmland and reduce the need for new land.

2.Multiple Cropping: Multiple cropping is a method of growing two or more crops on the same land in a single year. This method can help to increase the productivity of existing farmland and reduce the need for new land. Multiple cropping can also help to improve soil fertility and reduce soil erosion.

3.Land Reclamation: Land reclamation is the process of converting wasteland or swampy areas into productive farmland. This can be done by draining the land, adding soil amendments, and introducing crops that are suited to the soil and climate of the area. Land reclamation can help to increase the amount of land available for cultivation.

4. Land Consolidation: Land consolidation is the process of merging small and fragmented land holdings into larger and more productive farms. This can be done by exchanging land between farmers, creating new access roads, and improving irrigation systems. Land consolidation can help to increase the productivity of existing farmland and reduce the need for new land.

5.Vertical Farming: Vertical farming is a method of growing crops in vertically stacked layers using artificial lighting and controlled environments. This method can help to increase the productivity of existing farmland and reduce the need for new land. Vertical farming can also help to conserve water and reduce the use of pesticides and fertilizers.
